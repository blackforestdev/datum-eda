The compound mark

Flick toward Align, then toward a direction — the two strokes join into one bent gesture (an inverted "L"). Because the sub-wheel always spawns in the same place relative to the parent, the whole shape becomes one memorized flick, drawn without the menu ever showing. That's the exponential speedup Kurtenbach & Buxton measured.

Spawn-in-place, not re-center

The submenu opens as its own radial at the flicked wedge's location (Sketchbook Pro), and the parent fades/blurs in place behind it — so the gesture flows outward and the path stays constant. Sub-wheels stay 4-wide cardinal (Left/Right/Top/Bottom) for reliable blind selection.
