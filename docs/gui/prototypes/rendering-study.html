Datum·EDA · Rendering Study

Modernizing symbols & footprints without breaking the standard

A design study for the Datum Rendering Book, in the shipped board-editor visual language. The look is re-skinned — filled symbol bodies, rounded copper, teardrops, real typography — while every standard-governed invariant (IEEE 315 topology, IPC land-pattern metrics) stays exact. Nothing here is decorative: what you see is what the CAM emits.

Law 1 — Fidelity
One geometry, screen & fab

The renderer and the Gerber/drill/paste exporter consume the same derived geometry — gated. Every manufacturable mark on screen is byte-for-byte what the fab receives. Only glow/grid/ratsnest are screen-only.

Law 2 — Default
Beauty is the resting state

Rounded pads, teardrops, DFM-optimal miters and thermal reliefs are default-on, rule-driven per net-class. A clean board is the engine's output — not something the user grinds to force (the KiCad tax).

02

Footprint — precision made visible

SOIC-8 · IPC-7351B
Land pattern · copper substraterounding ratio 25% · pitch 1.27mm
Copper pad (roundrect) Component body Courtyard Silkscreen Dimension / origin
Rounded-rectangle pads at a 25% rounding ratio — the corner radius is real, manufactured geometry: it raises copper peel strength, etches clean, and solders without bridging. The sharp-cornered pad is the fiction (the etch rounds it anyway). The silkscreen outline is inset from the pads to hold the IPC silk-to-copper clearance — the assembly outline may abut the copper, the silk may not. Gold copper, courtyard hairline, pad-1 marker, origin crosshair and cyan dimension lines — the board-editor palette, at footprint scale.
03

The DFM Geometry Solver — author topology, derive geometry

naïve vs Datum default
Naïve — as drawnsharp · acute · butt junction
Acute inside corner traps etchant; the 90° bend adds corner capacitance & current crowding; the butt-jointed pad risks annular-ring breakout.
Datum — derived defaultchamfer · miter · teardrop
You drew the same centerline. The engine derived the acute chamfer, the Douville–James miter (M% = 52 + 65·e^(-1.35·W/h)), and the teardrop from trace width, angle & net-class — rule-driven, undoable, through commit().
04

Silkscreen type — the fab fingerprint

the "you can always tell Eagle" tell
Centerline stroke fontEagle / legacy
A single-width centerline skeleton. Legible, but no weight, no contrast, no hand — the instantly-recognizable low-grade tell.
Filled outline typefaceIBM Plex · outline
Real outline glyphs — IBM Plex (the project's typeface), rendered as filled silk polygons that respect the silk minimum feature width. And the on-screen silk is the same geometry the fab receives — no separate pretty path (Law 1). The signature, carried into fab & assembly docs.
05

Icon contact sheet

24px · 1.7 stroke · round caps
UI glyph systemgated by icon_set.json
Every glyph is one declared entry in icon_set.json — 24px grid, 1.7 stroke, no fill, round caps & joins. The gate forbids an undeclared icon; Phase 1 renders missing ones via a declared fallback so the shell never blocks on art.
