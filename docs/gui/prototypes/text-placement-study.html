Datum·EDA · Text & Placement Study

Text scales to the part — the tool advises, never dictates

A study for the Rendering Book's text chapter. Silk text has no forced minimum height: the only hard limit is what the fab can physically image; legibility is a soft hint you can turn off. Reference text is a movable label tethered to its device. All type is IBM Plex, rendered at true relative scale below.

01

Sizing scales to the part

true relative scale · 1 mm = 44 px
Real parts, real silk0201 · 0402 · SOIC-8
The 0402's "R1" is 0.5 mm; the 0201 is 0.30 mm. A 0.8 mm floor would print text taller than the part. Datum lets each designator fit its part — down to whatever the fab can image.
The only hard line is the fab, not legibilityheight ladder
Only below the fab's imageable feature is a hard error. The 0.8 mm legibility line is a soft hint, off by default — you won't dismiss it every DRC run the way KiCad makes you.
02

Clearspace — a minimum distance to its neighbours

keep-clear = 0.5 × cap height (H)
Clear — neighbour respects the gapgap ≥ 0.5·H
Keep-clear is a distance, not a box: a neighbour — here another silk label, C7 — must sit at least 0.5 × the cap height (H) away. Because it's a ratio of H, the gap scales with the text automatically. C7 sits right at the line — clear.
Crowded — soft warninggap < 0.5·H
When a neighbour comes closer than 0.5·H it's flagged as a soft text_overlap warning — a legibility nudge, distinct from the hard silk-to-copper rule. Advice you can ignore, never a gate.
03

Moving text is moving a label

tethered to its device · nets untouched
RefDes / value = a movable field owned by the partdrag → tether
A designator is a field owned by its footprint/symbol — nine auto-positions plus free drag. Nudge it and a tether shows what it belongs to (like every EDA tool). The move is journaled (undo/provenance) but can't touch your netlist — it's a label, not a connection.
04

Schematic text on the grid

1.27 mm (50 mil) default
Grid-anchored, nudgeable off-gridIBM Plex Sans
Everything lands on the 1.27 mm (50 mil) grid — ref, value, pin name, net label — matching the tool your users know, and finely nudgeable off-grid when a sheet gets tight. Pin numbers use IBM Plex Mono (they're data); names and labels use Sans.
